Bridging food rescue gap is a simple but important idea:

👉 It is the difference between how much surplus food is available and how much of it is actually rescued and redistributed to people in need.

🍽️ What does it mean in real life?

Every day:
Restaurants, hotels, weddings, supermarkets, and homes waste large amounts of edible food
At the same time, many people struggle to get even one proper meal

The gap exists because: 
➡️ Food is available

❌ But it does not reach the people who need it

🔍 Why does this gap exist?

1. Lack of coordination
Donors (restaurants, events) don’t know who to give food to NGOs.Don’t know where surplus food is available.

2. Time sensitivity
Cooked food spoils quickly
Delay in pickup = food becomes unusable

3. Logistics issues
No proper transport or storage (like refrigerated vans)
Small NGOs lack infrastructure

4. Awareness problem
Many people don’t even know they can donate food safely

5. Legal and safety concerns
Fear of food poisoning liability
No standard guidelines followed everywhere

⚖️ Impact of the food rescue gap

🍛 Massive food waste (tons of food thrown daily)

😔 Hunger and malnutrition continue

🌍 Environmental damage (wasted food → methane gas in landfills)

💸 Economic loss of resources (water, labor, energy used to produce food)

💡 Simple Example
Imagine:
A wedding has 200 extra meals
Nearby slum has 100 hungry people
If no one connects them → food is wasted

👉 That disconnect = food rescue gap

🛠️ How to reduce the food rescue gap?

1. Technology solutions
Mobile apps to connect donors and NGOs (like food rescue platforms)

2. Real-time tracking
Notify nearby volunteers instantly when surplus food is available

3. Better logistics
Local delivery networks, bikes, or volunteers

4. Awareness campaigns
Encourage restaurants and households to donate

5. Policy support
Government laws to protect food donors (Good Samaritan laws)
